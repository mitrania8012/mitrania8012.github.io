Steak Diane

A Gordon Ramsay Recipe
〜

  • 🍲 Feeds: 4
  • 🕑 Active time: 30 min
  • 🕑 Total time: 30 min

Ingredients:

  • 4 small sirloin steaks, about 7 oz.
  • 3 peeled shallots or pearl onions
  • 4 ounces mushrooms of choice- sliced and cut into thin strips
  • 1 tablespoon salted butter
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Salt and Pepper
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/3 cup brandy or cognac
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Small handful of flat-leaf parsley
  • Olive oil

Recipe:

  • Cut fat off of
    steak
    , flattening with rolling pin if needed
  • Season steak using
    salt and pepper
  • Heat
    olive oil
    in a pan
  • Sautee steaks for two minutes, flipping halfway through, then allow them to rest
  • Heat some
    olive oil
    in another pan
  • Add
    shallots/onions
  • Wait one minute, then add
    mushrooms, butter, garlic, Worcestershire sauce,
    and
    mustard
  • Turn up heat and pour in
    alcohol
    - fire will light
  • Once alcohol is gone, move the juice around the pan, then add
    heavy cream
  • Wait until sauce is thick and then turn off the sauce
  • Reintroduce steaks to the other pan and allow them to cook
  • Serve steak on plate, spooning sauce on top

Cooking is about passion, so it may look slightly temperamental in a way that it's too assertive to the naked eye. -Gordon Ramsay

Steak & Mushrooms

A Cooking Light Recipe
〜

  • 🍲 Feeds: 4
  • 🕑 Active time: 35 min
  • 🕑 Total time: 45 min

Ingredients:

  • 4 beef tenderloin steaks (4 oz, 1 inch thick)
  • Salt & Pepper
  • 3 minced garlic cloves
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped shallots or pearl onions
  • 1 pound sliced mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine (such as Pinot Grigio)
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce

Recipe:

  • Rub
    steaks
    with
    salt, pepper,
    and one
    minced garlic clove
  • Melt
    butter
    in a heated non-stick pan (medium high)
  • Add steak, cook for 3 minutes each side
  • Remove steaks from pan, keeping them warm
  • Add
    onions/shallots
    to pan
  • Wait until onions brown, about 4 minutes, stirring occasionaly
  • Add
    mushrooms
    and cook with onions until the onions caramelize
  • Add remaining
    garlic
    and sautee for 30 seconds
  • Introduce
    wine, Worcestershire sauce, salt
    and
    pepper
  • Cook until wine is almost completely evaporated, around 3 minutes
  • Remove mixture from stove
  • Serve warmed steaks on plate, add 1/4 of sauce to each one

Cooking is like painting or writing a song. Just as there are only so many notes or colors, there are only so many flavors - it's how you combine them that sets you apart. -Wolfgang Puck

Bell Pepper Steak

An Epicurious Recipe
〜

  • 🍲 Feeds: 4
  • 🕑 Active time: 60 min
  • 🕑 Total time: 80 min

Ingredients:

  • 2 top-sirloin steaks, 1.5 pounds each
  • 6 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 6 minced garlic cloves
  • 1 large sliced white onion
  • 2 large bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 cup unsalted beef or chicken broth
  • Handful minced parsley

Recipe:

  • Place
    steaks
    in gallon ziplock
  • Add
    vinegar, 1/2 of garlic,
    and
    2/3 of oil
    to the bag
  • Shake to coat the steaks and let rest, room temperature, for half an hour
  • While steak marinates, heat remaining
    olive oil
    over medium heat (use heavy skillet)
  • Add
    onion, bell peppers,
    and
    garlic
  • Sautee for 8 minutes
  • Add
    broth
    and increase heat, boiling until vegetables are tender and liquid is thick (5 min)
  • Season with
    salt
    and
    pepper,
    then keep warm
  • Prepare barbeque or broiler
  • After steaks are done, sprinkle both sides with
    salt
    and
    pepper,
  • Grill both steaks, about 6 minutes each side
  • Let steaks rest while vegetables are brought to a simmer
  • Slice each steak in half, serving it on a plate
  • Add vegetables and juices in skillet to serve, sprinkle with
    parsley

Taste as you go. When you taste the food throughout the cooking process you can make adjustments as you go. -Anne Burrell
